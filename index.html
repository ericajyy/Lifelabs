Background of the story


Story Setting

The story takes place in Daxing, the capital city of the Sui Dynasty, located in modern-day Xi’an, China. This setting is during the early 7th century, a time when the Sui Dynasty was in the midst of major infrastructure projects likes the construction of the Grand Canal and enduring constant military campaigns. These endeavors placed immense pressure on the common people, leading to social unrest, which becomes a critical element in the story.



Traditional Framework

During the Sui Dynasty, the culture was deeply rooted in Confucianism, patriarchal values, and a strict social hierarchy. The family unit and societal order were structured around clearly defined gender roles, especially the role of women, who were expected to be subservient to men, manage household duties, and preserve family honor through obedience and virtue.

Summary Tale


Storyboard

Fei Lin, a talented woman in the Sui dynasty, feels trapped by strict gender roles. Her father arranges her marriage to Shi Wang, a conservative man. Although she excels in literature and philosophy she suppresses her intelligence to honor her family. While Shi Wang is away, Fei Lin learns about a rebellion led by shu xian against oppressive taxes and gender roles. Intrigued, she secretly joins the movement but her husband is tasked with suppressing it. When captured, Shi Wang confronts her, feeling betrayed. In his moment of anger, he takes her life. After her death the rebellion is crushed but Fei Lin’s sacrifice inspires many women to question their roles in society.



Closing with Hope


After Fei Lin's Tragic Ending

Fei Lin at the end dies, but her death wasn't in vain; it made women at their time question their role in society which helped them to be recognized and respected.
